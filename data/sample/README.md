# data/sample — Pipeline Trace Reference

Committed sample showing the full input→output chain for a single claim through the pipeline. Use this to understand what the pipeline produces without running it yourself.

## `pipeline_trace.json`

End-to-end lifecycle of **clm_0037** ("WIV was conducting gain-of-function research in BSL-2 conditions") — the top-scoring crux (score 0.61):

| Stage | Event Type | What Happens |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| 1. Extraction | `claim.asserted` | Claim extracted from source with mandatory quote. `quote_verified: true`. |
| 2. Edges | `edge.asserted` | 29 edges detected (23 supports, 3 contradicts, 2 qualifies, 1 frames_differently) |
| 3. Confidence | `claim.rank_changed` | Confidence score computed from evidence combination × quality product |
| 4. Settling | `meta.flag` | Pipeline detects verdict claims that depend on this unresolved crux |
| 5. Position | `position.stated` | Claim assigned to a position via HDBSCAN + graph community detection |
| 6. Challenge | `challenge.raised` | External counter-evidence added, triggering re-evaluation cascade |

## What to look for

- Every claim has `relevant_quote` — exact text from source document
- Every edge has `evidence` — LLM explanation of why the relationship exists
- The `cross_source` field on edges shows whether claims come from different sources
- The settling flag shows `contested_cruxes` and `severity` computed from the dependency chain
