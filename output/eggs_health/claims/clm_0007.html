Claim Detail

For people with diabetes, eating one or more eggs a day was associated with increased heart disease risk, and it may be best to limit egg yolk consumption to no more than three per week.

Source & Provenance

"In studies including the Nurses’ Health Study and Health Professionals Follow-up Study, heart disease risk was increased among men and women with diabetes who ate one or more eggs a day. For people who have diabetes and heart disease, it may be best to limit egg consumption to no more than three yolks per week."

Source: Egg consumption and risk of cardiovascular disease: three large prospective US cohort studies · Original

Belongs To

This position uniquely argues that, despite mixed evidence, limiting egg consumption—particularly egg yolks—is advisable for people with diabetes to reduce cardiovascular risk.
