This position uniquely argues that, despite mixed evidence, limiting egg consumption—particularly egg yolks—is advisable for people with diabetes to reduce cardiovascular risk.

This position emphasizes that evidence suggests a link between high egg consumption and increased heart disease risk in diabetics, advocating for moderation or limitation of egg yolk intake. It acknowledges conflicting studies but prioritizes the potential risk associated with eggs, especially in the context of diabetes management.

Core commitment: High egg intake may increase cardiovascular disease risk in individuals with diabetes, warranting dietary restrictions on eggs.
