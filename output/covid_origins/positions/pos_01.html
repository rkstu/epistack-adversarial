This position uniquely argues that the evidence strongly favors a laboratory origin of COVID-19, emphasizing genetic anomalies, laboratory research activities, and statistical improbabilities associated with zoonotic spillover, thereby challenging the mainstream zoonosis hypothesis.

This position contends that the unique genetic characteristics of SARS-CoV-2, such as the fully formed furin cleavage site and the context of laboratory research, point to a lab leak origin. It challenges the zoonosis hypothesis by highlighting statistical improbabilities and the likelihood of laboratory accidents, asserting that the preponderance of evidence favors a laboratory source over natural spillover.

Core commitment: The most fundamental claim is that the genetic features of SARS-CoV-2, particularly the furin cleavage site and the proximity of the outbreak to the Wuhan Institute of Virology, are more consistent with laboratory manipulation or accidental release than natural emergence.
