This position uniquely argues that the risks associated with high-energy particle collisions, such as those at the LHC, are negligible or overstated because the probability of dangerous phenomena like black hole or strangelet formation is extremely low or effectively impossible at the energies involved.

This position contends that the scientific and probabilistic assessments demonstrate an exceedingly low likelihood of catastrophic outcomes from LHC experiments. It emphasizes that higher energies reduce the chances of dangerous strangelet formation and that existing calculations show the probability of black hole creation is virtually negligible, thereby dismissing fears of doomsday scenarios.

Core commitment: The probability of catastrophic events resulting from LHC collisions is so minuscule that such risks can be confidently dismissed.
