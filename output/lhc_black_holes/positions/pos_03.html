This position asserts that legal systems and courts have consistently dismissed safety-related lawsuits against the LHC, demonstrating that such legal challenges lack credible evidence and are unfounded.

This position emphasizes that multiple courts, including the European Court of Human Rights and the German Constitutional Court, have rejected safety-related lawsuits against the LHC, underscoring the lack of credible evidence for safety risks. It argues that legal challenges based on safety fears have been consistently dismissed, reinforcing confidence in the LHC's safety and the legitimacy of its operation.

Core commitment: Legal authorities have found no sufficient grounds to halt or challenge the operation of the LHC based on safety concerns.
