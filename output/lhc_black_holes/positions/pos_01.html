This position uniquely argues that the existence of extra dimensions could lead to observable phenomena at the LHC—such as microscopic black holes and missing energy signals—challenging the standard view that such phenomena are impossible or highly unlikely within current physics models.

This position emphasizes that theories involving extra dimensions predict new, detectable phenomena at the LHC, such as microscopic black holes and graviton emissions, which could provide evidence for physics beyond the Standard Model. It challenges the conventional view that such phenomena are either impossible or too speculative to be observed with current technology.

Core commitment: Extra spatial dimensions exist and have measurable effects at high energies, enabling phenomena like micro black holes and graviton emissions that are not predicted by the Standard Model alone.
