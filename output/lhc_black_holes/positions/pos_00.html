This position uniquely argues that the safety concerns regarding black hole production at the LHC are scientifically unfounded and have been conclusively addressed by extensive research and expert consensus, emphasizing the natural occurrence of similar high-energy events in the universe without catastrophic consequences.

This position asserts that comprehensive scientific assessments, including peer-reviewed reports and expert reviews, have definitively shown that the LHC cannot produce dangerous black holes or other hazardous phenomena. It emphasizes that natural cosmic events with higher energies have occurred safely over billions of years, and that the scientific community and institutions like CERN have publicly affirmed the safety of the collider, thereby dismissing any doomsday concerns.

Core commitment: The LHC poses no danger of creating stable or dangerous black holes, as such scenarios have been thoroughly evaluated and dismissed by scientific studies and expert authorities.
